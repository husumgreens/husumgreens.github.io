1. INTRODUCTION

1.1. SUMMARY OF GUIDELINES

This document contains the architectural guidelines and standards for the Husum Greens subdivision. Pursuant to Article 7 of the Declaration, the Architectural Review Committee ("ARC") adopts the below-listed Architectural Standards Manual and Guidelines governing each Lot built or improved after plat recordation.

While individual Owners retain the right to design and build a home consistent with their needs and choices, it is also important that certain standards and guidelines are in place to ensure that there is general architectural consistency from Lot to Lot, and that guidance can be provided to Owners as they seek to build on their Lot or make modifications to an existing Improvement.

To this end, the following Architectural Standards Manual and Guidelines are to be used by the ARC in evaluating plans and specifications under the Declaration concerning those Improvements, including structures and landscaping. It is the responsibility of the ARC to review an Owner's submittals with specific reference to the following standards and guidelines.

The standards and guidelines recognize that each Lot may present certain unique circumstances which should be taken into account by the ARC in administering the standards and guidelines, but that a general architectural and aesthetic appearance for Husum Greens also needs to be established and maintained. While cost considerations to an Owner can be taken into account by the ARC in reviewing plans and specifications, cost alone should not be a principal consideration for the ARC in its review.

All terms used in the standards and guidelines shall have the defined meanings set forth in the Declaration. The ARC retains all rights to amend, modify, or revise the following standards and guidelines.

1.2. THE PURPOSE OF THESE GUIDELINES

The specific objectives of this document are:

  1. To illustrate basic design principles, which will aid Owners in developing exterior improvements and structural changes that are in harmony with the immediate neighborhood and community as a whole.
  2. To provide uniform guidelines to be used by the ARC in reviewing applications in light of the goals set forth in the governing documents of the community and the actions of the Board of Directors.
  3. To assist Owners in preparing an acceptable application to the ARC.

To increase Owners' awareness and understanding of the Declaration.

To describe the organization and procedures involved with the architectural standards.

To provide guidelines that integrate the site's natural elements with residential home sites.

To assist Owners in maintaining a well-kept community in order to protect and enhance the economic, property, and aesthetic values, and the overall desirability of the homes within the community.

1.3. THE USE OF THESE GUIDELINES

Each application is to be reviewed on an individual basis before any project is scheduled to begin. The following types of projects need approval by the ARC:

  1. Construction of homes on unbuilt Lots;
  2. Additions or alterations to structures, if visible from the exterior of the structure;
  3. Installations of Decks, Fences, Patios, Porch screening, Driveways, or Walls;
  4. Repainting a structure in a different color, if the color is not on the palette;
  5. Landscaping that involves changes in Lot elevations, removal of trees, plants that may block a street view, creating a potential safety concern, or addition of shrubs or trees that constitute a natural fence; or
  6. Addition of sheds, lean-tos, or other accessory buildings.

1.4. DESIGN REVIEW CRITERIA

The following criteria outline the general standards that will be used in reviewing and evaluating such application for structural or design changes.

  1. Validity of Concept: The basic idea must be sound and appropriate to its surroundings.
  2. Design Compatibility: The proposed Improvement must be compatible with the architectural characteristics of the applicant's house, adjoining houses, and the neighborhood setting. Compatibility is defined as similarity in architectural style, workmanship quality, and similar material usage, color, and construction details. Because of recent significant advances in panelized and modular construction techniques, customized designs incorporating these methods will be evaluated on a case by case basis.
  3. Building Classification: Only residential properties are allowed on Lots.
  4. Location and Impact on Neighbors: The proposed home or any subsequent alteration should relate favorably to the landscape, the existing structure when applicable, and the neighborhood. The primary concerns include, but are not limited to, access, sunlight, ventilation, and drainage. For example, (a) fences may not obstruct views, breezes, or access to a neighboring property; (b) decks or larger additions may not cast unwanted shadows on an adjacent patio or property or infringe on a neighbor's privacy; and (c) lighting may not cast unreasonable light on or to adjacent properties. The size of the proposed alteration should relate well to adjacent structures and their surroundings. For example, a large addition to a small house or construction of a storage outbuilding may be inappropriate.
  5. Color: Color may be used to soften or intensify visual impact. Similar portions of an addition to an existing house, such as roofs, siding, and trim, should be matching in color. Paint colors for shutters, entry doors, and garage doors must be one of the approved colors for the community or one approved by the ARC. Wooden trim (e.g., eaves, fascia, window framing) and gutters/downspouts must be approved by the ARC.
  6. Materials: When proposing to extend or modify an existing home, continuity is established by use of the same or compatible materials as were used in the original home. The options may be limited by the design and materials of the original home.

1.5. MUNICIPAL REGULATORY PRECEDENCE

All plans and specifications submitted to the ARC must be fully compliant with all legal standards, including those of Klickitat County. The ARC shall have no authority to make any exceptions to such standards. Any ARC decision which is subsequently found to be in conflict with such standards shall be voided, requiring the Owner to conform, at the Owner's expense, to such legal standards.

2. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

This section defines acceptable new building materials. A licensed contractor is required, and a copy of the license must be submitted prior to commencement of construction.

2.1. Building Sizes

No structure shall exceed 28 feet in height, measured from the highest point of finished soil immediately adjacent to the foundation to the highest point of roof structure. No residential dwelling shall be less than 1,500 square feet excluding the garage/carport or have a foot print larger than 5,000 square feet including an attached garage. Each Lot shall be limited to one detached structure, which shall not be more than 30% of the square footage of the primary residence, and which foot print may not be more than 1,000 square feet regardless of primary residence square footage. Every primary residence shall be required to contain at least one enclosed garage or detached carport capable of containing 2 full sized cars.

2.2. Trim and Siding Colors

All exterior colors must be approved by the ARC. Owners are encouraged to use local rocks or replica colors and textures. No Lots are allowed to incorporate bricks of any color. Exterior colors shall be natural stain with a satin a finish, not a gloss finish. Paints shall be earth tones with a flat or satin finish.

2.3. Siding

Maximum reveal for any lap siding will be 8 inches with a minimum of 4 inches. Siding material may be wood, cementitious fiberboard, synthetic plaster, brick or stone veneer, or as otherwise approved by the ARC.

2.4. Roof and Roof Design

Architectural styles for Lots are allowed to incorporate hip roofs, gabled roofs, and flat or drop panel flat roofs. Architectural styles for all other Lots shall have chalet style roofs. All finished roofing shall be either Architectural 80 asphalt tab roofing, ceramic tile, or metal roofing that meets the approved colors/tones for the community.

2.5. Windows

Wood / metal clad wood, vinyl, and thermally broken aluminum windows will be approved. All windows must have exterior trim installed. The exterior color of draperies, under draperies, drapery lining, or other window coverings for any building shall not be any color other than earth tones, unless the Board grants a waiver.

2.6. Exterior Lighting

All exterior lighting shall conform to the Klickitat County Dark Skies Ordinance.

2.7. Fireplaces

Wood or pellet burning fireplaces, stoves, or similar devices are required to have and maintain catalytic systems and cinder screens. Tanks for propane storage shall be buried or installed in a below-ground vault and must follow IBC and NFPA codes and regulations.

2.8. Driveways

No driveway shall have a slope greater than 15%. No driveway shall have a width less than 14 feet or a width greater than 22 feet, except within 30 feet of the face of : garage or carport, when the hard surface can be up to 40 feet wide or across the entire face of the garage plus 12 feet. All driveways are required to have a hard surface composed of either paver stones, poured concrete, or asphalt from the public street to the garage apron.

3. EXTERIOR AND SITE REQUIREMENTS

This section defines acceptable exterior and site-specific regulations in addition to those regulated by the Declaration.

3.1. Excavation

Excavation of more than 50 cubic yards for any purpose must be approved by the ARC.

3.2. Fences & Walls

The design and placement of any fences and walls, including retaining walls, shall require the approval of the Declarant or the ARC.

3.3. Fire Zone

Owners must maintain a fire safety zone of 30 feet around all structures on flat ground and 40 feet on slopes greater than 6%. The fire safety zone should adhere to the following measures:

  1. Tree limbs on trees with a DBH (diameter at breast height) of at least 12 inches must be raised 7 feet from the ground.
  2. Grass must be kept under 6 inches in height.
  3. No dead standing trees.

3.4. Exterior Fire Protection

Owners are prohibited from burning yard debris, garbage, or other combustible material at any time. The Board shall make available a chipping machine at least 3 times per calendar year for the purpose of debris disposal.

3.5. Landscape Irrigation

Use of domestic water for landscape material is prohibited except for the initial establishment of landscaping outside the irrigation season. All other exterior landscape irrigation shall only be provided with the supplied irrigation system. Owners must install and maintain irrigation flow sensors which ensure monitoring of correct flow to avoid water waste and excess run-off. Owners are required to install and maintain pressure-regulated spray heads for irrigation.

3.6. Application of Herbicide, Pesticides, and Fertilizers

To the extent practicable, the Association should restrict the broadcast application of herbicides, and should confine applications to point/plant specific applications. To the extent practicable, the Association should avoid broadcast or large-scale disbursement of pesticides, and should confine application to specific areas of infestation. The Association shall avoid the application of fertilizers within 25 feet of wetlands, drainage channels, storm water retention structures, and ponds. Application of fertilizers in other areas under the control of the Association shall follow the Manufacturer's Instructions.

3.7. Off-Site Housing

No frame-mounted off-site constructed manufactured housing is allowed. Off-site constructed modular housing is allowed.

3.8. Solar

All primary residences are allowed to install either roof top or ground-based solar systems with the set-back requirements found in the County Zoning limits, and subject to final approval by the ARC.

4. LANDSCAPING REGULATIONS

This section defines acceptable new landscaping materials in addition to those allowed by the Declaration.

4.1. Timing Constraints of Landscaping

All disturbed soils are to be planted or landscaped within 90 days of occupancy or completion of any structure or improvement. Landscape plans shall be submitted to the Declarant or ARC 30 days prior to the commencement of planting, and shall be subject to the approval of the board.

4.2. Exterior Maintenance by Owner

Each Lot, the home and all Improvements on the Lot, and the landscaping of the Lot, shall be maintained by the Owner in a neat, clean, and sightly condition at all times, and shall be kept free of accumulation of litter, junk, containers, equipment, building materials, and other debris. The landscaping of each Lot shall be kept in a neat and sightly condition including mowing, trimming, pruning and other standard practices in accordance with such rules or regulations adopted by the Board. The yards on each Lot shall reflect reasonable maintenance consistent with the neighborhood, including, but not limited to, the following:

  1. Grass should not grow over sidewalks, curbs, walkways, or driveways.
  2. Flower beds, shrubbery beds, planting areas, and other non-lawn areas shall be kept free of weeds and grass.
  3. Underlying ground cover, such as plastic sheeting, should not be visible.
  4. Plastic sheeting or other similar materials may not be used as a top-layer ground cover.
  5. Shrubbery should be pruned so as not to encroach upon the street.
  6. Trees should be pruned so as not to impede pedestrian or vehicle ingress and egress along the street. Trees overhanging into adjacent Lots should be maintained by the Owner of the Lot where the tree is planted. Trees and other shrubs may not unreasonably interfere with light or ground conditions of adjacent Lots.
  7. A Lot shall not be used as a dump for trash or rubbish of any kind. Household trash, lumber, building materials, vehicle parts, inoperable vehicles, abandoned or broken equipment, apparatus or devices, grass clippings, branches, and other yard refuse may not be left on front or side yards, sidewalks, streets, or other areas where they are visible from the street or any other Lot.

4.3. Landscaping Nuisances

No Lot shall be used in whole or part for storage of anything that in the opinion of Declarant, the Board, or the ARC will cause a Lot to appear in an unclean, disorderly, or untidy condition. An Owner shall not undertake, permit, or allow any noxious activity or thing on any Lot that, in the opinion of the Board or the ARC, may be or become a nuisance or unreasonably interfere with the use and enjoyment of other Lots by any Owner. An Owner shall keep all Lots owned by the Owner prior to, during, and after the construction of the home and all Improvements in a neat and orderly condition free of brush, vines, weeds, trash, and debris, and shall maintain all Lots owned by the Owner in such condition of maintenance and repair to prevent the creation of a nuisance or health hazard. Garbage cans and recycling containers shall be screened from view.

4.4. Fences, Walls, and Hedges

Lots may not have fences or walls on the street boundary of the Lot. Chain link fences are not permitted. Hedges up to a height limit of 4 feet are acceptable. The hedge species requires prior ARC approval. The side and rear boundaries of the Lots may be, but are not required to be, fenced, walled, or hedged. No hedge, fence, or wall shall extend further forward than the dwelling's front elevation upon the Lot. The height limit is 6 feet on the rear boundary and 5 feet on the side boundary. The fence structure must extend at least 2 feet beneath ground level, with posts made of cedar, rot-resistant 4x4s, or better.

5. CONSTRUCTION PROCEDURAL REQUIREMENTS

5.1. Responsibilities of the Owner

  1. The Owner must have clear title to make property improvements, notwithstanding mortgages.
  2. The Owner should refer to both this document and any other governing documents of the Association for architectural requirements.
  3. The Owner and subsequent Owners agree to indemnify, defend, save and hold harmless the Association and its officers, agents and employees from any claim, suit, action or activity undertaken under these Standards, including construction under an approved building permit.
